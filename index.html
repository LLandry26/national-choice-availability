Eligibility by Statute

Eligibility is defined by statute based on student eligibility criteria set under current program law. Common restrictions include household income limits (often expressed as a percentage of free-and-reduced-lunch thresholds or the federal poverty limit), student characteristics, family household location, prior public school enrollment status, and other criteria.

Max Capacity

Once eligibility is established, participation is subject to program limitations, generally tied to available state funding or available contributions to state programs. In some cases, additional limits cap the maximum number of participants allowed per year even among eligible students.
